fwupd is a system daemon that makes firmware updates on Linux automatic, safe, and reliable. It integrates with the [Linux Vendor Firmware Service (LVFS)](https://fwupd.org/) to deliver signed firmware updates for hundreds of device types — from UEFI capsules and NVMe drives to USB peripherals and Thunderbolt controllers.

## What fwupd does

fwupd runs as a background daemon and exposes a D-Bus interface. End users interact with it via `fwupdmgr` on the command line, or through graphical frontends like GNOME Software, GNOME Firmware, and KDE Discover. Hardware vendors publish firmware to the LVFS, and fwupd downloads, verifies, and installs updates automatically.
